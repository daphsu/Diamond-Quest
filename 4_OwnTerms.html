“How is it possible that so much of contemporary life feels so arbitrary and so inescapable? …. It feels like a trick, a trick that has worked and is still working, that the bride remains the image of womanhood at its most broadly celebrated—and that planning a wedding is the only period in a woman’s life where she is universally and unconditionally encouraged to conduct everything on her own terms.”


—Jia Tolentino, “I Thee Dread” in Trick Mirror
